1 Introduction
The field of 3D content generation has evolved from optimization-based reconstruction to
zero-shot synthesis via Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) [1] and Score Distillation Sampling
(SDS) [2]. While subsequent architectural refinements in Magic3D [3] and Fantasia3D [4]
have significantly improved visual resolution and surface extractability, these models remain
hindered by a fundamental lack of 3D geometric awareness inherent in 2D diffusion priors.
This deficiency leads to persistent "geometric hallucinations," most notably the Janus problem,
where objects display redundant or inconsistent features [2], [5], [6] . Theoretical analysis
identifies these artifacts as a manifestation of "mode collapse," where optimization settles into
high-probability 2D frontal views [5]. The current state-of-the-art approach, Hallo3D [7],
attempts to mitigate this by using Large Multi modal Models (LMMs) to detect structural errors
and generate "enhanced negative prompts." However, a critical research gap remains - LMM
driven frameworks suffer from secondary reasoning hallucinations, where the LMM
misidentifies errors or fails to perceive subtle spatial inconsistencies. Furthermore, relying on
a purely text-based feedback loop creates a "semantic bottleneck," as negative prompts lack
the spatial precision required to guide 3D optimization effectively, often resulting in over-
correction or new artifacts.
The significance of addressing these multi layered hallucinations is vital for the professional
scalability of AI driven asset creation in Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), and
gaming, where structural integrity is as critical as visual fidelity. Current frameworks fail
because they cannot ground LMM reasoning into precise 3D spatial constraints.
This research project introduces a closed loop geometric grounding framework designed to
overcome the reasoning limitations and secondary hallucinations identified in Hallo3D [7].
The proposed technology moves beyond simple textual prompts by integrating LMM detection
with a direct, spatially aware geometric feedback mechanism. By translating multi-modal
observations into explicit structural constraints, this research aims to eliminate both primary
2D prior hallucinations and secondary LMM reasoning errors, providing a robust
methodological solution for consistent and structurally sound 3D content generation within the
AI literature.

to zero-shot generative synthesis. The technical foundation was established by Neural

as continuous volumetric functions. While NeRF excelled at view synthesis, it required multi-
view supervision. The leap to text-to-3D was facilitated by DreamFusion [2], which introduced

3.2 Defining 3D Hallucinations and the Janus Problem
In 3D generative literature, "hallucination" refers to the emergence of geometrically
inconsistent or semantically redundant features that do not exist in the source prompt [8]. The
most pervasive hallucination is the Janus Problem, characterized by the duplication of
canonical features (e.g., multiple faces or sets of limbs) [2], [5], [6]. This occurs because 2D
diffusion models are biased toward high-probability frontal views in their training data. As
noted by Wang et al.[5], this is a manifestation of "mode collapse," where the optimization
settles into a local minimum of a specific 2D viewpoint. Additionally, texture
hallucinations often manifest as over-saturation or "color bleeding," a byproduct of the high
guidance scales required for convergence in models like TextMesh [9] and Latent-NeRF [10].
3.3 Evolution of Structural Fidelity and Disentanglement
To improve geometric accuracy, researchers moved beyond simple volume
density. Magic3D [3] utilized a coarse-to-fine strategy with hash grids to achieve higher
resolution, while Fantasia3D [4] introduced a disentangled framework using Physically Based
Rendering (PBR) and surface normals to refine geometry separately from appearance.
Furthermore, recent breakthroughs in 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) [11] have offered faster
rendering speeds, yet these explicit representations remain vulnerable to the same 2D prior
induced hallucinations. Variational Score Distillation (VSD), introduced in ProlificDreamer
[12], attempted to solve consistency by modeling the 3D scene as a distribution rather than a
single point, though it increased computational complexity significantly.
3.4 Mathematical Mitigation and Score-Space Debiasing

to the score space. Perp-Neg [6] proposed a perpendicular gradient sampling method, ensuring
that negative prompts (e.g., "side view") do not interfere with the primary semantics of the
frontal prompt. Similarly, Entropic Score Distillation (ESD) [5] introduced an entropy

3.5 The Multi-Modal Gap - Analyzing Hallo3D and its Limitations
The most recent shift involves the use of Large Multi-modal Models (LMMs) to simulate
human-like reasoning. Hallo3D [7] introduced a "generation-detection-correction" paradigm,
leveraging models like GPT-4V to identify structural hallucinations (e.g., "a bird with two
beaks") and generating "enhanced negative prompts" to guide the diffusion process.
However, critical analysis of [7] reveals a significant open challenge - Multi-modal Reasoning
Hallucinations. Current LMMs are not natively 3D-aware and often provide inaccurate
feedback or fail to perceive subtle geometric errors that cannot be easily verbalized.
Furthermore, Hallo3D relies on a semantic bottleneck; it converts a visual error into text, which
is then re-interpreted by a 2D diffusion model. This often leads to "over-correction" or the
introduction of new artifacts that the LMM cannot track in real-time.
3.6 Rationale for Proposed Research
Existing research has moved from pure 2D priors [2] to mathematical debiasing [5], [6] and
text-based multi-modal detection [7]. Yet, the field lacks a closed-loop system that grounds
LMM reasoning into spatially aware geometric constraints. This proposed research aims to
overcome the limitations of Hallo3D [7] by replacing the semantic bottleneck with a direct
visual geometric feedback mechanism. By integrating LMM detection with explicit 3D
structural priors, this project will address the "secondary hallucinations" found in multi-modal
pipelines, positioning the contribution as a definitive solution for consistent, structurally sound
3D content generation.
4 Problem Definition / Research Gap
To address the "semantic bottleneck" and reasoning hallucinations in current text-based 3D
generation loops (e.g., Hallo3D [7], Magic3D [3]), this research proposes replacing imprecise
"negative prompts" with direct geometric grounding. This feedback mechanism aims to
provide the spatial precision required to eliminate structural inconsistencies and ensure
topological integrity.
5 Proposed Research Outcome

5.3 Methodology and Architecture
ΓÇó Generative Pipeline - SDS-based synthesis of initial SDF-mesh from text/single-image
input using 2D diffusion priors.
ΓÇó Multi-View Renderer - Produces diverse 2D projections for spatial analysis.
ΓÇó LMM Hallucination Detector - Large multimodal model scans renderings for structural
issues (e.g., Janus faces, view inconsistencies).
ΓÇó Adversarial Geometric Critic - Translates LMM feedback into a differentiable 3D
energy function that localizes anomalies in spatial coordinates/regions.
ΓÇó Grounded Refinement Loop - Iterative optimization adds geometric energy as
adversarial loss to directly refine 3D parameters (SDF/mesh), enforcing manifold
consistency without text re-prompting.
5.4 Process Flow
ΓÇó Input - A text prompt triggers the base 3D synthesis.
ΓÇó Detection - The LMM detects hallucinations.
ΓÇó Mapping - Critic maps detections to 3D energy function (localizes issues).
ΓÇó Refinement - Optimizer refines geometry via combined SDS + geometric loss until
artifacts vanishes.
ΓÇó Output - Clean, consistent, photorealistic 3D asset.

7 Summary
This research introduces Adversarial Geometric Distillation (AGD) to mitigate hallucinations
in text-to-3D generation, focusing on geometric artifacts like genus inconsistencies and blob
problems caused by mode collapse in score distillation frameworks. By integrating an auxiliary
adversarial critic (GNN-based for topology detection) with grounded self-distillation (teacher-
student refinement using Laplacian metrics), AGD replaces view-biased 2D priors with
explicit spatial constraints, yielding consistent, manifold-sound 3D meshes without relying on
imprecise negative prompts or external multi-modal reasoning. The outcome is a novel,
efficient pipeline that reduces Janus artifacts and enhances topological accuracy, as validated
on datasets like Objaverse.
